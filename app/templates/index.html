The Project

Our Code Detectors team is planning on developing an AI system that generates argumentative essays based on the different parts of an essay (counter, claim, rebuttal, evidence and conclusion) after receiving a short prompt. We want to pursue this project for educational purposes so that people can quickly and easily dive into topics that are interesting to them. We will be using a dataset called Evaluating Student Writing from Kaggle to train our AI system.

Our Process

We decided to assign all of our team members roles and once those were determined we started our work. First, we began our project by searching for a data-set. Next, part of our team went ahead with the pre-processing and data training. Once we were finished with training we went ahead and started working on our front-end. We collaborated and discussed with our whole team how we were envisioning our website design to look. Once we agreed on a template we could begin our HTML and CSS coding. The back-end worked on connecting to the front-end.

Challenges

It was extremely difficult to find a dataset to train the model How did we fix it? We found one dataset that was similar to what we wanted so we slightly changed our project to include the subject of the essay (such as claim, conclusion, evidence, rebuttal, etc) to match the dataset We also had to change that it would only generate one part of the essay based on the subject type that was given in the prompt.

What Model We Choose And How Long It Took To Train

We chose GPT-2 NEO and AI textgen. We assigned two people from our team to work on training our model. The claim, counterclaim, and conclusion statements each took approximately 20 minutes to train. The evidence and rebuttal statements took around 30 minutes each to train. In the end, the whole model took 2 hours to train.

Our Goals

Our goal is to make an easy-to-use interface for a user to gain knowledge about their prompt and make essay writing more accessible. We are aware that many struggle with writing essays, so our hope would be that Essays.arg() can help make essays less intimidating and easier to write. We definitely would not want to overwhelm the user, so our whole team is working on making our Essay generator as friendly as possible.

Future Cases And Expansions

Currently we are only focusing on argumentative type of essays and statements. Our system will be able to generate claims, counterclaims, rebuttals, evidence, and conclusion statements. However, in the future we intend to increase our scope to different type of essays such as descriptive, persuasive, etc. This would allow our system to generate other types of statements such as descriptions, comparisons, and more.
